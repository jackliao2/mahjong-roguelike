2026 ROUNDUP

10 Best Online Mahjong Games to Play in 2026

From competitive riichi clients to cozy casual apps, here are the best places to play mahjong online this year, plus the best trainer for sharpening your skills.

Whether you want ranked riichi matches, casual play with friends, or short practice sessions, the right platform depends on the rules and experience you want. This guide compares ten online mahjong options by ruleset, learning support, device access, multiplayer format, and cost so you can quickly narrow the field.

#1 BEGINNER PICK

Mahjong Soul

Platform: Web, PC, MobileStyle: Riichi

Mahjong Soul is the most polished riichi mahjong client available in English. It has tutorials, ranked modes, events, and a thriving community. The interface teaches you the rules as you play, and the matchmaking keeps beginners away from sharks.

Best for: New players who want a complete, friendly introduction to riichi mahjong with competitive depth.
#2 COMPETITIVE

Tenhou

Platform: Web, PCStyle: Riichi

Tenhou is the legendary Japanese riichi mahjong client. It is bare-bones, fast, and filled with strong players. The ranking system is respected worldwide. The interface is in Japanese, but English guides exist and the gameplay is universal.

Best for: Serious players who want to test themselves against high-level competition.
#3 MOBILE RIICHI

Riichi City

Platform: Mobile, WebStyle: Riichi

Riichi City brings riichi mahjong to mobile with anime-style characters and smooth touch controls. It is more accessible than Tenhou and has a growing global player base. The ranked ladder gives you meaningful goals to chase.

Best for: Players who want competitive riichi on their phone.
#4 SOLO TRAINER

Mahjong Quiz

Platform: Web BrowserStyle: Quiz / Training

Mahjong Quiz is our own free browser trainer. It turns real mahjong situations into 4-option quiz questions covering yaku form, waiting tiles, best discards, and hand reading. The roguelike chapter format makes practice feel like a game.

Best for: Beginners who want to build pattern recognition quickly without the pressure of live matches.
#5 LEARNING AI

Kemono Mahjong

Platform: PC, MobileStyle: Riichi

Kemono Mahjong is a single-player riichi mahjong game with a strong AI and excellent teaching tools. It explains why discards are good or bad, making it one of the best learning environments for solo players.

Best for: Solo learners who want detailed feedback on every decision.
#6 CHINESE CLASSIC

Mahjong Time

Platform: Web, MobileStyle: Multiple

Mahjong Time offers Chinese, riichi, and other regional variants. The player base is smaller than Mahjong Soul, but the variety is unmatched. If you want to try different mahjong styles in one place, this is it.

Best for: Players curious about Chinese or international mahjong variants.
#7 CASUAL MATCH-3

Mahjong Journey

Platform: Mobile, WebStyle: Solitaire

Mahjong Journey is a tile-matching puzzle game, not real mahjong. It is relaxing, beautiful, and perfect for passing time. It will not teach you riichi strategy, but it will sharpen your tile-matching eye.

Best for: Casual players who want a relaxing puzzle experience with mahjong tiles.
#8 FRIENDS & FAMILY

Mahjong Club

Platform: MobileStyle: Chinese / Casual

Mahjong Club focuses on private tables and social play. You can create rooms, invite friends, and play without worrying about ranked stress. The rules are customizable, which is great for house variants.

Best for: Groups of friends who want to play together online.
#9 CLASSIC SOLO

Microsoft Mahjong

Platform: PC, Mobile, WebStyle: Solitaire

Microsoft Mahjong is a polished solitaire tile-matcher with daily challenges and themed boards. Like Mahjong Journey, it is not the four-player game of mahjong; choose it for a relaxed matching puzzle, not for learning riichi strategy.

Best for: Casual Windows users who want a familiar, reliable puzzle game.
#10 VR EXPERIENCE

Mahjong VR

Platform: VR HeadsetsStyle: Riichi / Social

Mahjong VR puts you at a virtual table with other players. The novelty of physically moving tiles is genuinely fun, and the social presence is better than flat-screen clients. The player base is small but passionate.

Best for: VR owners who want a social, immersive mahjong experience.

How to Choose the Right Game for You

If you are new to riichi mahjong, start with Mahjong Soul. It is the most welcoming entry point and has the largest English-speaking community. If you want serious competition, move to Tenhou once you understand the basics. If you prefer to learn alone, Kemono Mahjong is excellent.

No matter which game you play, your progress will accelerate if you also train offline. Live games are great, but they do not give you focused repetition. That is where Mahjong Quiz comes in. Use it to drill the skills that live games only test occasionally.

Why Training Matters More Than Playing

Playing real games is fun, but it is an inefficient way to learn. In a full match, you might only face ten meaningful decisions. In the same amount of time, a quiz trainer can give you fifty. If you want to improve fast, combine real play with deliberate practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best online riichi mahjong game for beginners?

Mahjong Soul is the best starting point for most beginners. It has a clean interface, tutorials, ranked matches, and a large English-speaking community. The game handles scoring automatically so new players can focus on strategy.

Is Tenhou free to play?

Yes, Tenhou is free to play in its basic form. It is a no-frills Japanese riichi mahjong client with a highly competitive player base. The interface is older and less beginner-friendly than Mahjong Soul, but it is excellent for serious practice.

Can I practice mahjong without playing against real people?

Yes. Mahjong Quiz is a free browser-based trainer that turns mahjong situations into 4-option quiz questions. It is ideal for practicing yaku recognition, waiting tiles, best discards, and hand reading without the pressure of a live match.

What is the difference between Chinese mahjong and riichi mahjong online?

Chinese mahjong usually uses 144 tiles with flowers and seasons, and scoring is often simpler. Riichi mahjong uses 136 tiles, features the riichi bet, dora multipliers, and a more complex yaku system. Our how-to-play guide explains riichi rules in detail.

Are there mahjong games on mobile?

Yes. Mahjong Soul, Riichi City, and Kemono Mahjong all have mobile versions. Mahjong Quiz also runs in any mobile browser, so you can train on the go.

Final Thoughts

The best mahjong game is the one you actually play. If ranked riichi excites you, Mahjong Soul and Tenhou are unbeatable. If you want to learn at your own pace, Kemono Mahjong and Mahjong Quiz are perfect companions. And if you just want to match tiles and relax, the solitaire games on this list are excellent.

Whatever you choose, remember that mahjong rewards patience. The strongest players are not the ones who memorize the most yaku. They are the ones who practice the fundamental skills until they become automatic. Pick a platform, play regularly, and use a trainer to fill the gaps. That is how you improve in 2026.
