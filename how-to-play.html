SECTION 01

Quiz Basics

Mahjong Quiz is a browser-based learning game. Instead of drawing and discarding tiles against opponents, you answer multiple-choice questions about riichi mahjong hands. Each question shows a hand and four tile options. Pick the right tile to advance.

The goal is simple: answer every question correctly and climb as high as you can. One wrong answer ends the run (unless you have a spare life). Questions get harder as you progress, but so do the rewards.

Difficulty Modes

Pick a mode before each run:

  • BEGINNER — 8 questions, 2 lives. Starts with tenpai questions and gently introduces yaku, waits, and discards.
  • NORMAL — 12 questions, 1 life. Harder questions, tighter timer, bigger score potential.
  • ENDLESS — unlocked after NORMAL. Questions keep coming with increasing difficulty until you miss one.

Lives & Game Over

Wrong answers cost a life. Run out of lives and the run ends. Your score is recorded on the local leaderboard so you can chase your personal best. The "one wrong answer" tension is what gives the quiz its roguelike feel — every choice matters.

Quick tip: Read the prompt carefully. A question might ask which tile wins, which tile completes a yaku, which tiles you're waiting on, or which tile is the safest discard. The same hand can have different correct answers depending on the prompt.

SECTION 03

Chapters & BOSS Questions

Questions are grouped into chapters of three. The third question in each chapter is a BOSS question:

  • Highlighted with a red border.
  • Worth +1,500 bonus points when answered correctly.
  • Often combines two skills, such as forming a yaku while finding the right wait.

Clearing a BOSS question unlocks a relic draft. If you miss a BOSS, you lose a life and the run continues if you have lives left.

Chapter flow: Question 1 → Question 2 → BOSS → Relic draft → Path choice → Next chapter. This loop repeats until you finish the mode or run out of lives.

SECTION 04

Relics & Paths

After each BOSS, you choose one of three relics. Relics give small advantages for the rest of the run, such as extra time, a hint highlight, or bonus points for speed.

Hint Scroll

Highlights one wrong option per question. Great when you're unsure.

Time Charm

Adds extra seconds to the timer for questions that need more thought.

Combo Emblem

Boosts your combo multiplier faster for consecutive correct answers.

Score Lens

Increases base points per correct answer.

After the relic draft, you choose a path:

  • SAFE PATH — easier questions, standard score multiplier.
  • RISKY PATH — harder questions, higher score multiplier.

Pick the path that matches your confidence and your remaining lives. Low on lives? Play safe. Chasing a high score? Take risks.

SECTION 05

Scoring & Combos

Each correct answer adds points. Several factors multiply your score:

  • Base points — higher in later chapters and on BOSS questions.
  • Combo — consecutive correct answers build a combo, up to +100% bonus.
  • Speed bonus — answer quickly for extra points.
  • Path multiplier — risky paths multiply every question's score.
  • Relics — some relics add flat bonuses or improve multipliers.

SECTION 06

Beginner Strategy

Here are five habits that will improve your quiz score immediately.

1. Read the Prompt First

Don't scan the hand until you know what the question is asking. The same hand can have different answers for tenpai, yaku, wait, and discard prompts.

2. Count the Blocks

A winning hand needs five blocks: four melds + one pair. Count them mentally. If a hand has only four blocks, it's one tile away from ready.

3. Prioritize Ryanmen Waits

A two-sided wait (like 4-5 waiting on 3 or 6) covers eight tiles. Edge waits and single-tile waits cover far fewer.

4. Keep Simples for Tanyao

Tiles 2-8 are "simples." Terminals (1, 9) and honors break Tanyao. When the target yaku is Tanyao, avoid terminals and honors.

Ready to apply these tips? Start a run and learn by doing. Every answer includes an explanation, so even mistakes teach you something.

Mahjong Quiz FAQ

Is the mahjong quiz good for beginners?

Yes. The quiz format teaches riichi mahjong one concept at a time. BEGINNER mode starts with simple tenpai questions and gradually introduces yaku, waits, and discards. You learn by answering, not by memorizing a rulebook.

How long does a quiz run take?

BEGINNER runs have 8 questions and take 5-10 minutes. NORMAL runs have 12 questions and take 10-15 minutes. ENDLESS mode continues until you answer one wrong.

What happens if I answer a question wrong?

One wrong answer costs one life. BEGINNER gives you 2 lives; NORMAL gives 1 life. Run out and the run ends. Your score is saved locally.

Do I need to know mahjong to play?

No. The first chapters teach tenpai, tanyao, and pinfu through the questions themselves. Each answer includes an explanation so you learn why a tile is correct.

What is the difference between this and a real mahjong game?

This is a practice tool, not a full 4-player match. There are no opponents, no wall, and no calls like pon or chi. The focus is on recognizing patterns, waits, and efficient discards.
