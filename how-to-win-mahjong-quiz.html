How to Win at Mahjong Quiz: Pro Tips for High Scores

Chapters, boss questions, relics, combos, and the endless ladder. Here is everything you need to push your score higher in Mahjong Quiz.

Mahjong Quiz combines decision training with a short roguelike run. Hand knowledge determines whether you find the answer; combo management, relics, paths, adaptive difficulty, and optional score pressure determine how far you can push the run.

Understand the Nine Question Types

Mahjong Quiz tests nine related skills. Each question type rewards a different reading process, so high scores require more than memorizing a few common hands.

  • Tenpai-win: Identify whether a hand is complete and which tile wins it.
  • Yaku-form: Recognize which yaku a hand qualifies for.
  • Waiting-tiles: Name the tile or tiles a tenpai hand is waiting on.
  • Discard-best: Choose the discard that maximizes efficiency or safety.
  • Ukeire-choice: Compare the verified number of live improving tiles after each discard.
  • Table-decision: Use placement, turn, value, wait quality, and opponent pressure to choose riichi, dama, push, or fold.
  • Multi-wait: Find hands that wait on multiple tiles in complex shapes.
  • Yaku-combo: Identify which yaku stack together in a single hand.
  • Safe-discard: Pick the safest tile to discard against an opponent's riichi.

Each type uses the same 4-option format, but the mental process is different. Tenpai-win is about completeness. Yaku-form is about memorization. Discard-best is about efficiency. Safe-discard is about defense. Master them one at a time.

Chapter Mode: Learn Before You Race

Chapter mode is the heart of Mahjong Quiz. Each chapter introduces question types gradually and ends with a boss question. Think of chapters as skill gates. Do not rush through them.

If you are stuck on a chapter, the problem is usually one skill, not the whole game. Go back and practice that question type in isolation. For example, if boss questions keep beating you on waiting tiles, read our waiting tiles guide and drill that mode specifically.

Relic Strategy: Build for Your Weakness

Relics are the roguelike layer of Mahjong Quiz. They give you passive bonuses during a run. Some add score multipliers. Some extend the timer. Some protect your combo or reveal hints. The best relic choice depends on what is holding you back.

If you run out of time often, pick timer relics. If you make careless mistakes, pick combo-protection relics. If you are confident but slow, pick score multipliers. A common mistake is choosing flashy relics that do not match your actual weaknesses. Be honest about where you lose points and build accordingly.

Combo and Score Multipliers

Your combo streak is your most important scoring asset. Each correct answer adds to the combo, and many relics scale with it. A long combo can turn an average run into a high-score run.

This means consistency beats speed. It is better to answer carefully and maintain a streak than to rush and break it. If a question feels risky, use the time you have. A wrong answer resets your combo and often costs more than the time you saved.

Choose Your Read: Steady or Press

Before advanced efficiency, defense, and table-state questions, you may choose Steady Read for normal scoring or Press the Table for a 1.6x score multiplier. Pressing adds 25% opponent risk if the answer is wrong or the timer expires.

Press when you recognize the pattern and can explain the answer before clicking. Choose Steady when the situation depends on unfamiliar notation or close placement math. After a wrong press, the multiplier resets before a retry, so guessing cannot be used to farm the higher reward.

Boss Questions: Slow Down

Boss questions are harder versions of normal questions. They often involve multi-wait shapes, unusual yaku combinations, or safe-discard puzzles with multiple opponents. The biggest mistake players make is panicking.

Slow down. Apply a system. For hand-reading questions, count the blocks first. For yaku questions, check the obvious yaku before the exotic ones. For safe-discard questions, use genbutsu first, then suji, then kabe. If you have a hint relic, this is the time to use it.

PRO TIP

Pre-Read the Question Type

Before the options appear, identify what the question is asking. Is this a wait question or a yaku question? Is it offense or defense? Pre-reading lets you activate the right mental framework before you evaluate the choices.

Why it works: Each question type uses different patterns. Switching into the right mode faster reduces errors and saves time.

Timer Management

The timer adds pressure, but it also rewards decisiveness. The key is to spend time where it matters. Easy questions should be instant. Hard questions deserve a few extra seconds. Boss questions deserve your full attention.

If you find yourself consistently running out of time, the issue is usually recognition speed, not thinking speed. Drill the same question type repeatedly until the patterns become automatic. Speed comes from familiarity.

Endless Mode: The Real Test

Endless mode is where high scores live. The questions get harder over time, and one mistake can end a great run. Use endless mode after you have cleared the relevant chapters. It is a stamina test, not a learning environment.

In endless mode, relics that stabilize your run become more valuable than relics that spike your score. Combo protection, extra time, and hint charges help you survive longer. Survival is what produces the highest scores.

Study Outside the Game

The quiz is a training ground, but it is not the only place to learn. When you miss a question, ask why. If it was a yaku question, review our yaku list. If it was a hand-reading question, read our hand reading guide. If it was about efficiency, study our tile efficiency guide.

The best players do not just play more. They study the gaps the quiz reveals. Treat every wrong answer as a signal pointing to a specific skill you need to improve.

Mindset for High Scores

High scores come from calm consistency, not heroic plays. Do not chase big combos with risky guesses. Do not panic on boss questions. Do not get frustrated by wrong answers. Each run is a chance to practice patterns.

Track your progress informally. Are you getting further in endless mode than last week? Are you missing fewer waiting-tile questions? Those improvements matter more than any single score.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the seven question types in Mahjong Quiz?

Mahjong Quiz uses nine question types covering winning tiles, yaku, waits, discards, ukeire, table-state decisions, multi-waits, yaku recognition, and defense. Adaptive difficulty introduces harder reads after a streak and recovery questions after repeated misses.

How does the combo system work?

Each correct answer adds to your combo streak. Higher combos multiply your score and can charge relic abilities faster. A wrong answer breaks the combo, so consistency matters more than speed for long runs.

What are relics in Mahjong Quiz?

Relics are meta-upgrades that give you bonuses during a run. Some add score multipliers, others extend the timer, and some reveal hints or protect your combo. Choosing relics that match your weakest skill is usually the best strategy.

How do I beat boss questions?

Boss questions are harder versions of normal questions, often with multi-wait shapes or unusual yaku combinations. Slow down, count the blocks, identify the yaku, and double-check your wait before answering. Using a relic that adds time or removes wrong options helps a lot.

Is endless mode better than chapter mode for practice?

Chapter mode is better for structured practice because it introduces question types gradually. Endless mode is better for testing your stamina and pushing for high scores. Use both: chapters to learn, endless to master.

Final Thoughts

Winning at Mahjong Quiz is not about being the fastest clicker. It is about building reliable pattern recognition and managing the roguelike systems wisely. Study the question types, choose relics that cover your weaknesses, protect your combo, and stay calm under the timer.

The quiz is a mirror. It shows you exactly which mahjong skills need work. Use it honestly, and your real-game play will improve alongside your scores. That is the real win.
